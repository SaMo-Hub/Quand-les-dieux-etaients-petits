GENALOGIA

The studio lights flicker once, then surrender completely. The hum of computers dies, leaving only the sound of your collective breathing and the faint creak of old timber. Someone jokes that the ghosts in your trendy 18th-century chapel studio have finally claimed their due. You try to rally the team, but half the group’s already checking their phones for signal, the other half just stare into the dark. A power company says they’re working on the problem. You read it three times. It feels like a lie.
